I, Ronald Schlifman, M.D., hereby swear the following to be true to the best of my knowledge:

 

1.       I am a physician duly licensed to practice in the State of New York.

2.       I have personally reviewed the peer report written by Isandr Dumesh, M.D. dated September 5, 2016, who determined that the MRI performed on the claimants right/left shoulder on July 14, 2016 was not medically necessary.

3.       I submit this affirmation of medical necessity on the basis of the documentation provided, medical records and the peer review supplied, in order to come to a conclusion of whether the MRI prescribed was required and medically necessary for the claimants pain and injuries. 

4.       I have read and reviewed the peer review report and denial by {INSURANCECOMPANY_NAME}, bills from {PROVIDER_NAME} and all the relevant medical records.

5.       {INJUREDPARTY_NAME}, a 31-year-old male/female was a restrained driver/pasanger of a vehicle involved in a motor vehicle accident on 2/13/2016. As a result of impact, he/she sustained multiple injuries, including injuries to his/her right/left  shoulder. Following the accident, the patient was taken to the emergency room of Brookdale Hospital where he/she was evaluated, treated, performed CT scan of his head and released for an outpatient care. He/She subsequently developed complaints of pain and presented to Dr. Albert Ciancimino on 07/12/2016 for an initial evaluation of his/her injuries. At that time, the patient complained of neck, middle back, low back and right shoulder pain. Upon examination, the patient was found to be in moderate distress due to pain and discomfort. Examination of the right/left shoulder revealed tenderness to palpation with limited range of motion.

             Based on the aforementioned complaints and findings upon evaluation, Dr. Ciancimino     reached a preliminary diagnosis of sprain/strain and contusion of the right/left shoulder. Dr. Ciancimino therefore recommended the patient to start on a course of physical therapy, chiropractic evaluation, and referred for acupuncture treatment. He also referred for Orthopedic consultation. Dr. Ciancimino also prescribed various diagnostic tests including MRI studies of the right/left shoulder. He also prescribed some medical supplies.

 

The patient then started on a course of conservative treatment. He/She also had MRI studies of the right/left shoulder.

 

MRI of the right/left shoulder performed on 07/14/2016 revealed distal subscapularis tendonosis.

 

Based upon a review of aforementioned documents, taking into consideration the patients history, the history of the injury, the patients complaints, the clinical findings and a review of the medical history, and in accordance with the generally accepted standards of care in the relevant medical community, the MRI of the right/left  shoulder performed on 07/14/2016 was medically necessary, within a reasonable degree of medical certainty.

 

6.       Dr. Isandr Dumesh denied the medical necessity of the aforementioned studies based on his conclusions in the peer review report. I respectfully disagree with his conclusions for the following reasons:

               With respect to MRI of the Right/Left Shoulder:

 

Dr. Dumesh stated that MRI should be done in a cases requiring ruling out significant ligament damage, when results of the test would be incorporated into a treatment regimen. In this case, Dr. Ciancimino referred the patient for an MRI of the right/left shoulder to r/o rotator cuff tear injury. As evidence from the patient's medical records, examination of the right shoulder revealed tenderness to palpation with decreased range of motion. American College of Radiology Website, Guidelines for the performance and interpretation of MRI of the shoulder, revised, 2010, on page 2 and part II "Indications" (A) states: primary indications for an MRI of the shoulder include but not limited to, diagnosis, exclusion and grading of suspected: Rotator cuff tendon abnormalities: full thickness, partial thickness, and recurrent tears, tendinopathy, tendinitis, and cuff tear arthropathy."  According to this guideline MRI of the shoulder may be useful to evaluate specific clinical scenarios, including (not limited to) acute shoulder trauma, (present here) limited or painful range of motion (present here).

 

It is possible that the ultimate decision regarding the appropriateness of any specific radiologic examination or treatment must be made by the referring physician and radiologist in light of all the circumstances presented in an individual examination. (American College of Radiology (ACR) Appropriateness Criteria for MRI of Shoulder Trauma, National guideline Clearinghouse, U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, 2006).

7.       In conclusion, given the patients complaints, physical findings, reports and nature of the injury, the MRI testing of the right/left shoulder performed by {PROVIDER_NAME} on 07/14/2016 was medically necessary, and the findings further confirm and reinforce my opinion. Furthermore, the testing done on the patient is consistent with the generally accepted standards of medicine and professional standards within the medical community.
