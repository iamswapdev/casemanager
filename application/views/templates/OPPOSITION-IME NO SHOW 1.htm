AFFIRMATION IN OPPOSITION TO DEFENDANT’S MOTION

 

 

Alek Beynenson, an attorney duly admitted to practice law before the Courts of the State of New York, hereby affirms the following to be true under the penalties of perjury:

1.       I am a member of the law firm of The Beynenson Law Firm P.C., attorneys of record for Plaintiff, and as such am fully, familiar with the facts and circumstances surrounding this action from the records and files maintained therein.

2.       This affirmation is submitted in opposition to Defendant's motion to dismiss plaintiff’s complaint on the ground that plaintiff failed to attend all Examinations Under Oath ("EUO") and all Independent Medical Examinations (“IME”).

3.       Defendant’s motion should be denied in its entirety because: (i) it is incomplete and evades several concrete questions of fact, and (ii) it has failed to demonstrate the EUO requests and IME requests were actually mailed.  Further the defendant failed to demonstrate that the claimant did not actually appear for the IMEs.

I. DEFENDANT HAS FAILED TO FULFILL
THEIR BURDEN ON SUMMARY JUDGMENT

4.       It is well-settled that on a motion for summary judgment, the moving party has the initial burden of demonstrating, by admissible evidence, its right to judgment. The burden then shifts to the opposing party, who must proffer evidence in admissible form establishing that an issue of fact exists warranting a trial (Zuckerman v City of New York, 49 NY2d 557, 562; GTF Mktg. v Colonial Aluminum Sales, 66 NY2d 965,967-968; Singer v Friedman, 220 AD2d 574, 577; Frost v County of Rensselaer, 220 AD2d 969, 970).

5.       In this case, several issues of fact remain, warranting a trial. In its moving papers, the defendant has failed to sufficiently demonstrate that the cause of action at hand is devoid of merit. The affidavits offered within this motion fail to answer several relevant inquiries, and fail to resolve tribal issues such that the threshold requirement for summary judgment cannot be met. Therefore, the plaintiff respectfully requests this Honorable Court deny defendant's motion in its entirety.

II. DEFENDANT HAS FAILED TO DEMONSTRATE THE EUO AND IME VERIFICATION REQUESTS WERE ACTUALLY MAILED

6.       In order to support a defense based upon an alleged failure to appear for an EUO or an IME, the burden is on defendant to demonstrate prima facie: 1) The IME/EUO requests were actually mailed; and 2) plaintiffs assignor failed to appear for the purportedly scheduled IMEs/EUOs. See generally, Stephen Fogel Pychological, P.C. v. Progressive Cas, Ins. Co., 35 A.D.2d 720 (2nd Dept. 2006);  Chi Acupuncture, P.C. v. Kemper Auto and Home Ins. Co., 2007 N.Y. Slip. Op. 50352(U) (App.Term 9th and 10th Jud. Dists. 2007). However, merely annexing EUO appearance letters isinsufficient to meet defendant's evidentiary burden and defendant must demonstrate such requests were timely - and actually - mailed. See generally, Contemp. Med. Diag. & Treatment, P.C. v. GEICO, 6 Misc. 3d 137(A) (App. Term 2nd and 11th Jud. Dists. 2005);  See also, Boai Zhong Yi Acupuncture Services, P.C. v. New York Central Mutual Fire Ins. Co., 12 Misc. 3d135 (A)(App Term 2nd and 11th Jud. Dists. 2006); Clark v. Columbian Mut. Life Ins. Co., 221 A.D.2d 227 (1 st Dept. 1995); Function Supply v. Progressive Ins. Co., 9 Misc.3d 1123(A)(N.Y. City Civil Ct. 2005).

7.       Indeed, in order to demonstrate its IME/EUO requests were actually mailed, defendant must proffer either an affidavit from an individual that personally mailed the IME/EUO requests or an affidavit from an individual with personal knowledge of the mailing practices and procedures of defendant and that it was such individual's duty to ensure compliance with such practices and procedures. See, Contemp. Med. Diag. & Treatment, P.C. v. GEICO, 6 Misc. 3d 137(A) (App.Term 2nd and 11th Jud. Dists. 2005).

8.       In Contemp. Med. Diag. & Treatment, P.C. v. GEICO, 6 Misc. 3d 137(A) (App. Term 2nd and 11th Jud. Dists. 2005), plaintiff initiated an action against defendant to recover outstanding no-fault benefits. Thereafter, plaintiff moved for summary judgment. Defendant cross-moved for the same relief asserting it timely tolled the thirty day time period to issue its denial of claim form by allegedly mailing timely verification requests. The lower court rejected defendant's contention and entered judgment in favor of plaintiff and defendant appealed. In affirming the decision of the lower court, the Appellate Term held:

The assertion of defendant's no-fault claims examiner that it was the "usual and customary course of business at GEICO that all documents are mailed on the same day they are generated" was conclusory and failed to specify either that it was the duty of the claims examiner to ensure compliance with said office procedures or that the claims examiner had actual knowledge that said procedures were complied with. Since defendant's opposition papers did not contain an affidavit of someone with personal knowledge that its verification requests were actually mailed, or describing the standard office practice or procedures it used to ensure that such requests were properly addressed and mailed (citation omitted). Id. (Emphasis added.)

9.       Similarly, in Boai Zhong Yi Acupuncture Services, P.C. v. New York Central Mutual Fire Ins. Co., 12 Misc. 3d 135 (A)(App Term 2nd and 11th Jud. Dists. 2006), plaintiff brought anaction against defendant to recover outstanding no-fault benefits. Plaintiff moved for summary judgment and defendant opposed alleging it timely mailed verification requests. The lower court denied plaintiffs motion and plaintiff appealed. In affirming the decision of the lower court, the Appellate Term held:

In an affidavit annexed to defendant's opposition papers, defendant's claims examiner stated that "[i]t is [defendant's] business practice to mail all verification requests to the address the applicant lists on the bill on the same day the verification request is generated. In compliance with that policy on the day [that] each verification request was generated: 2/25/02, 4/01/02 and 3119102, the verification request was mailed to [plaintiff]." In addition, unlike the affidavit of defendant's claims examiner in the case of Contemp. Med. Diag. & Treatment, P.e. v Government Empls. Ins. Co. (6 Misc 3d 137 rAJ, 2005 NY Slip Op 50254[U] [App Term, 2d & 11th Jud DistsD, defendant's claims examiner further stated that he had "personal knowledge of [defendant's] office practices and policies and [is] responsible for ensuring that they are enforced. " Defendant's opposition papers thereby sufficiently established the timely mailing of the verification requests since the affidavit of defendant's claims examiner was from one with personal knowledge of the standard office practice used by defendant to ensure that its requests were properly addressed and mailed (citation omitted). Id. (Emphasis added.)

 

10.   Likewise, in Clark v. Columbian Mut. Life Ins. Co., 221 A.D.2d 227 (lst Dept. 1995), plaintiff initiated an action against defendant to recover the proceeds of a life insurance policy. Defendant moved for summary judgment and alleged the policy had lapsed and that it mailed plaintiff a notice of disclaimed for failing to make a payment. Defendant proffered the deposition testimony of an individual that purportedly had personal knowledge of mailing. The insurer's witness testified as to the defendant's regular practices and procedures. The lower court found defendant met its burden of demonstrating a presumption of mailing and plaintiff appealed. However, defendant failed to elicit testimony that the office practice was geared toward ensuring the likelihood such notices were actually mailed and the Appellate Division reversed and held:

Mr. Smith testified he had no contact with the Data Processing Department of defendant which generated the notices and had little knowledge as to how this was done. Further, prior to his preparation in the present law suit, he had never seen the computer print-out which listed the insured’s who were supposed to be sent notices. He also testified that he did not know whether anyone checked the printed notices to ensure that they corresponded to the computer print-out. Further, he said that he did not know whether the envelopes he took to the post office were correctly addressed or if they contained all of the notices that were supposed to be sent that day, and he never counted the number of envelopes to ensure that they corresponded to the number of envelopes indicated on the affidavit he signed. Although, therefore, defendant submitted testimony on the preparation and mailing of the notices, it did not include sufficient evidence to ensure that the notices mailed corresponded to and included all of the names on the computer print-out or the correct addresses of those individuals. Thus, there was no showing that anyone compared "the names on the mailing list with the names and addresses on the envelopes for accuracy, or whether anyone routinely checked that the total number of envelopes matched the number of names on the mailing list." Id. at 228-229. (Emphasis added.)

 

11.   Finally, in Function Supply v. Progressive Ins. Co., 9 Misc.3d 1123(A)(N.Y. City Civil Ct. 2005) plaintiff initiated an action to recover outstanding no-fault benefits against defendant. Defendant moved for summary judgment and plaintiff cross-moved for the same relief. Defendant alleged it mailed timely verification requests to plaintiff thereby tolling the statutory thirty day time period defendant had to deny plaintiffs claim. The Honorable Justice Howard Lane held plaintiff established its prima facie case, but defendant failed to demonstrate it mailed the verification requests rendering its denial untimely as a matter of law. Justice Lane further held:

In support of its motion defendant submits copies of the alleged verification request addressed to plaintiff, and proof of mailing of its request for verification. Specifically, defendant proffers the affidavit of Linda Phillips, a litigation representative employed by defendant who avers in her affidavit in support of defendant's motion for summary judgment that "[w]ith respect to the mailing of the . . . verification requests, my office mailed same in accordance with its normal practice and procedure, followed in the regular course of my office's business ... " She further avers that "[ s ]uch request includes the specific claim information and bears the date that it is printed. It is then placed in a bin for the daily 1: 15 p.m. collection by my office's internal mail room personnel. The same day, a mail room employee prepares a post paid envelope bearing the same address of the entity that submitted the claim and seals the verification request in the envelope. Also the same day, a carrier from United States Post Office collects, with the mail, the envelope containing the verification request form at 3:30 P.M." Additionally, she avers that "any verification request form that is placed in the bin for mail room collection after 1: 15 p.m. is collected during the next business day's internal mail collection." The court finds the assertions of defendant's litigation examiner conclusory and such assertions fail to specify either that it was the duty of the litigation examiner to ensure compliance with said office procedures or that the litigation examiner had actual knowledge that said procedures were complied with. Id. (Emphasis added.)

 

12.   In the instant case, defendant has failed to demonstrate the EUO requests were, in fact, mailed. The annexed affidavit of DODGE & MONROY, PC secretary, Virginia Gervasi, purports to include information as to the actual mailing of alleged requests. However, the affidavit fails to demonstrate personal knowledge as to whether the relevant documents in this case were actually mailed.

13.   Virginia Gervasi claims that after she typed the EUO letters, they were signed.  Further she states: “Thereafter, it was signed once the original was signed, I mailed the letter certified mail, return receipt requested and regular US mail to RONDELL WAGNER's attorney … and by certified mail return receipt requested and regular US mail to RONDELL WAGNER.”

14.   It is evident that the EUO notices were signed by one Peter Dodge, not Virginia Gervasi, and there is no explanation how Gervasi may have insured that once she collected the letters from Peter Dodge after they were signed, that these were the correct letters. 

15.   Further, Virginia Gervasi states that she mailed the original via certified and regular mail to both the claimant and claimant’s attorney.  Obviously that could not have been possible, and there is no indication what steps may have been taken that copies (if such were made) were true and accurate of the original.

16.   With regard to the IME notices, an affidavit of Susan Buoncore, an employee of MCMC, LLC, a company retained by defendant to mail IME notices, is insufficient to establish proper mailing.

17.    Susan Buoncore claims to be personally familiar with the standard practices and procedures with respect to the receipt and mailing of IME notices, as well as ensuring that all such documents are properly addressed and mailed.

18.   However, simply restating the company's uniform procedure regarding the mailing process does not, in any way, prove that the no-fault denials and/or verification requests were mailed to plaintiff.

19.   Susan Buoncore does not specify who is accountable for maintaining a mailing log that serves as proof of mailing of IME notices.

20.   In addition, Susan Buoncore neglects to mention which employee is responsible for delivering all outgoing mail to the direct custody of a United States Postal Service.

21.   Susan Buoncore fails to make any assertions on who created the requests, where the request letters are placed to be mailed, when they are picked up, or any policy in place to check that all mail placed in a bin to be mailed is actually picked up.

22.   Susan Buoncore does not discuss the business practices and procedures to ensure that all mailing steps are done correctly. She fails to demonstrate personal knowledge that plaintiff received proper notice of these examinations.

23.   Susan Buoncore assumes that the purported policies were performed, simply because they were in place at that time.

24.   She fails to offer any relevant statement detailing how he actively mailed or has any personal knowledge of the mailing of these specific requests.

25.   Nothing in Susan Buoncore affidavit establishes that the supposed mailing routine was followed in respect to the alleged denial of claim forms and/or verification request letters.

26.   Based on the discussion supra, it is clear defendant has failed to demonstrate it actually mailed the EUO and IME requests. Indeed, defendant has failed to annex an affidavit from an individual with personal knowledge of the facts. Defendant's contentions are conclusory, not in admissible form, and therefore, worthless.

27.   Accordingly, defendant's motion must be denied in its entirety. Assuming, arguendo, this Court determines defendant demonstrated the mailing of the EUO and IME requests - which it did not - defendant's motion should still be denied as defendant has failed to meet the second prong of the test discussed supra - nonappearance.

III. DEFENDANT HAS FAILED TO DEMONSTRATE PLAINTIFF'S
ASSIGNOR FAILED TO APPEAR FOR THE SCHEDULED IMEs

28.   As previously discussed supra, it is incumbent upon defendant to demonstrate plaintiffs assignor failed to appear for the scheduled IMEs. Indeed, defendant is required to proffer an affidavit from an individual with personal knowledge sufficient to demonstrate plaintiffs assignor did not appear. See generally, Stephen Fogel Pychological, P.C. v. Progressive Cas, Ins. Co., 35 A.D.2d 720 (2nd Dept. 2006); Chi Acupuncture, P.C. v. Kemper Auto and Home Ins. Co., 2007 N.Y. Slip. Op. 50352(U) (App. Term 9th and 10th Jud. Dists. 2007). Failure to provide such proof in admissible form renders defendant's position impotent and its motion must be denied.

29.   In Stephen Fogel Pychological, P.C. v. Progressive Cas, Ins. Co., 35 A.D.2d 720 (2nd Dept. 2006), plaintiff initiated an action against defendant to recover outstanding first part nofault benefits. Defendant moved to dismiss and plaintiff cross-moved for summary judgment. Defendant alleged plaintiff violated a policy condition by failing to appear for two scheduled EUOs. Plaintiff opposes, asserting defendant failed to demonstrate such notices were actually mailed and failed to show plaintiff did not appear. The lower court denied defendant's motion and granted plaintiff's motion for summary judgment. Defendant appealed. On appeal, the Appellate Term modified the decision of the lower court and denied plaintiff's motion for summary judgment. The parties cross-appealed. The Appellate Division affirmed and held:

 In support of its motion for summary judgment, Progressive was required to establish, prima facie, that it mailed the notices of the EUOs to Kim and that he failed to appear for the EUOs.Progressive failed to meet its burden by proof in admissible form, because it submitted no evidence from anyone with personal knowledge of the mailings or of the nonappearances. (citations omitted). Given Progressive's failure to meet its burden, denial of its motion was required without consideration of Fogel's opposition papers. Id. at 720 (Emphasis added.)

30.   Similarly, in Chi Acupuncture, P.C. v. Kemper Auto and Home Ins. Co., 2007 N.Y. Slip. Op. 50352(U) (App. Term 9th and 10th Jud. Dists. 2007), plaintiff initiated an action recover outstanding no-fault benefits. Defendant moved for summary judgment on the ground that plaintiff's assignor failed to appear for two independent medical exams. The lower court found defendant demonstrated it issues a timely denial of claim form and that the EUO requests were mailed. The lower court denied defendant's motion. In affirming the decision of the lower court, the Appellate Term held:

 While defendant herein established proper mailing of the EUO requests, it did not submit evidence in admissible form from anyone with personal knowledge of the nonappearances. Since defendant failed to meet its burden, its motion for summary judgment was properly denied. Id. (Emphasis added.) 

31.   In the instant case, defendant has failed to annex an affidavit from an individual with personal knowledge of the alleged nonappearance at the IMEs.

32.   The annexed affirmation of Raghava Polavarapu, M.D. attempts to demonstrate the alleged nonappearances.

33.   The furthest the affiant goes explain his affiliation with the alleged nonappearance is to state that the plaintiff failed to appear for the scheduled IMEs.

34.   This statement does not offer any substantial proof an alleged nonappearance and is therefore, nothing more than a conclusory generalization.

35.   There are no claims of personal knowledge made by Raghava Polavarapu, M.D. that plaintiff received proper notice of these examinations, nor that plaintiff’s assignor did not actually appear.

IV. THE EUO NOTICES WERE DEFECTIVE

36.   Under the Regulations and case law all IME/EUO notices must contain language explicitly stating that the claimant will be reimbursed for both reasonable transportation expenses and any lost earnings as a result of the IME/EUO.  Any notice that does not include such language is defective.

37.   The EUO notices, annexed as Exhibit H of defendant’s moving papers, do not contain the requisite notice regarding transportation expenses and lost earnings.  As such the notices are facially defective.

38.   As such, plaintiff respectfully requests that this Honorable Court summarily deny defendant's motion in its entirety.

 

WHEREFORE, it is respectfully requested that the Court deny Defendant's motion in full and grant any further relief it deems fit.
