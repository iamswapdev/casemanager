AN INSURER MAY NOT A VOID A POLICY AB INITIO BASED UPON

MATERIAL MISREPRESENTATION

 

Defendant’s Motion for Summary Judgment is based on an alleged fraudulent misrepresentation by {INJUREDPARTY_NAME}. Defendant claims that the address provided by Mr. Kendall in his application for insurance was a misrepresentation. The Second Department held that it is error to hold that a policy that was obtained using fraudulent misrepresentations could be void ab ignition. The court further states that Vehicle and Traffic Law 313 “supplants an insurance carrier’s common-law right to cancel a contract of insurance retroactively on the grounds of fraud or misrepresentation, and mandates that the cancellation of a contract pursuant to its provision may only be effected prospectively.” Insurance Co. of North America v. Kaplum 274 A.D.2d 293, *298,713 N.Y.S.2d 214,**217 (N.Y.A.D 2 Dept.,2000).

Even assuming that Defendant can defend this case on their allegations, Defendant failed to “adduce proof in admissible form sufficient to create a triable issue of fraud, having submitted no affidavit by anyone with personal knowledge of the investigation. Melbourne Medical P.C. v Utica Mutual Insurance Co., 2004 WL 1431320 (N.Y.Sup.App.Term). The allegations of fact by an attorney who does not allege such knowledge is unsubstantiated hearsay. I.d.  In the instant case, the only proof submitted by Defendant is not in admissible form and is wholly irrelevant. Further Defendant does not offer an affidavit of someone with personal knowledge of the investigation. The court found that mere unsubstantiated assertions or speculations are insufficient to establish fraud. Alvarez v. Prospect Hosp., 68 N.Y.2d 320,327 [1986]). Even if Defendant offers proof of the misrepresentation, Defendant has not proven that the misrepresentation was material. No misrepresentation in material, as defined by Insurance Law 3105(b),”useless knowledge by the insurer of the facts misrepresented would have led to a refusal by the insurer to make such contract.” N.Y. Ins Law 3105(b). Defendant does not state that it would have refused the contract. Therefore, a question of facts remains.

 

WHEREFORE, it is respectfully requested that the Court deny defendant’s motion in its entirety, together with such other and further relief as this Court deem just, proper and equitable.
