AGREEMENT:  LITIGATION AND ARBITRATION OF NO-FAULT INSURANCE CLAIMS

 

I, _PROVIDER_PRESIDENT_ (PRESIDENT), owner of __ (provider’s name) (hereinafter referred to as "Client"), hereby retain and authorize the Law Office of Victor Gomelsky, Esq. (hereinafter “Firm”) to represent Client on any and all matters related to the collection of outstanding no-fault medical bills, including but not limited to arbitrating and/or litigating said claims; furthermore, Client and Firm agree to the following:

 

1. Payment of fees.  Client will pay the index number fees ($45) and arbitration filing fees ($40) up front, or in the event that Firm advances said fees, Client shall reimburse Firm for such fees within 20 days of receipt of the invoice.  Firm will advance other fees associated with litigation, including but not limited to process server fees and notice of trial fees, ($40).  Client shall reimburse Firm for such costs and fees within twenty days of receipt of the invoice.  Such charges apply only for cases that are neither settled nor awarded judgment via trial and/or motion practice. If any invoice mentioned in this section is not paid within thirty days of receipt by Client, Firm is authorized to deduct the owed amounts from any moneys collected or awarded as a result of litigation of Client’s files.

2. Within a reasonable amount of time after checks are received by Firm and deposited in an Attorney’s Escrow bank account, pursuant to settlements or judgments involving Client’s claims, Firm shall forward a check to Client for Client's share of the proceeds recovered, less outstanding disbursements owed by Client to Firm, if any.

3. Firm Fees.  Firm is entitled to the statutory no-fault insurance attorney fees payable by the defendant insurance companies upon settlements or judgments involving Client’s claims.  Moreover: 

a. Firm will charge a retainer fee to Client of 10% percent of the principal amount recovered for each of Client’s bills that are settled by Firm or for which judgments and/or orders are rendered in Client’s favor. 

b. With respect to interest collected pursuant to litigation or arbitration, Client and Firm agree that Firm is entitled to half (50%) of such interest.

4. Any statutory Attorney's fees recovered shall not mitigate, reduce or release Client's obligation to pay fees to the Firm at the above rates. This retainer agreement shall supersede all prior agreements between the Client and the Firm, without regard to any prior retainer agreements or practices which conflict with the terms of this agreement.

5. Client hereby represents that for each bill and claim that it submits to Firm for collection and/or litigation services, there is a duly executed assignment of benefits form signed by the patient who received the medical services/testing/supplies/medication listed on the bill, and that said assignment of benefits substantially complies with the assignment language mandated by the New York no-fault regulations.

6.  Client further agrees to bill in accordance with the New York State Workers Compensation Fee Schedule.

7. In addition to the fees stated above, Client authorizes the Firm to charge Client one hundred and fifty dollars ($150) if the ability of the Firm to collect the principal billed, statutory attorney's fees and/or the reimbursement of costs expended is jeopardized by any of the following:

                a) the bills submitted are determined not to be in accordance the governing Fee   Schedule;

                b) failure by Client to cooperate with the Firm including, but not limited to, compliance  with discovery exchange and/or court-ordered and stipulated deposition appearances

                c) failure by Client to inform the firm in writing that bills submitted for litigation   was paid prior to being provided to Firm;

                c) waiver by Client of the right to reimbursement from the insurance carrier;·

                d) the Client's failure to be and remain properly licensed for the services billed;

                e) fraudulent conduct including filing of false claims;

                f) the retention of another firm/attorney/collection agency to collect on the same bill(s) given to Firm;

                g) naming on claim forms of the wrong insurance-carrier, or failing to name the proper insurance carrier along with the third party administrator of the submitted claims

                h) failure by Client prior to litigation or arbitration to submit to the insurance company items and information that was requested by said insurance company via timely and proper verification requests

                i)  a ruling/finding by the Department of Education, or a court or arbitrational board of proper jurisdiction, that Client is not incorporated in accordance with Article 15 of the New York Business Corporation Law.

The above mentioned charges stated in this section (Section “7”) must be paid by Client within thirty (30) days of notice by Firm regarding violation of the above subsections.  If the charges mentioned in this section are not paid within thirty days of receipt of notice, Firm is authorized to deduct the owed amounts from any moneys collected or awarded as a result of litigation of Client’s files.

8.  Client hereby waives and will hold the Firm harmless from any legal claims whatsoever for any statute of limitation problems resulting, in whole or in part, from: the Client having been represented by another attorney/firm/collection company for the same claims; from insurance claims turned over to the Firm more than five years after the date of service(s); claims billed to an out of state or incorrect insurance carrier, or a TPA without listing the proper insurer on the claim forms; or claims billed to MVAIC unless

the claim has been qualified for MVAIC coverage and it is denied for lack of medical necessity.

9.  The client further waives and will hold the Firm harmless from any legal claims whatsoever for any changes in the law or court policies which necessitate the re-filing of lawsuits and/or arbitrations based upon criteria regarding consolidation of multiple bills into a single lawsuit, including but not limited to single patient vs. multiple separate lawsuits.

10.  In the event that Client transfers its files from Firm to another attorney or law office, Client agrees that Firm is entitled to an attorney’s lien of 10% (ten percent) of the principal amounts collected by the substituting firm as a result of settlements, judgments and orders pertaining to the transferred files.  The only exception to this lien is in the event that the files are transferred as a result of suspension or disbarment of the principal of Firm.

                THIS AGREEMENT SHALL SUPERCEDE ANY PRIOR AGREEMENTS BETWEEN CLIENT AND FIRM, WITHOUT REGARD TO WHEN THE CLIENT'S WORK WAS BROUGHT TO THE FIRM.  ANY PERSON WHO KNOWINGLY, AND WITH INTENT TO DEFRAUD ANY INSURANCE COMPANY OR OTHER PERSON FILES AN APPLICATION FOR INSURANCE OR STATEMENT OF CLAIM CONTAINING ANY MATERIALLY FALSE INFORMATION, OR CONCEALS FOR THE PURPOSE OF MISLEADING, INFORMATION CONCERNING ANY FACT MATERIAL THERETO, COMMITS A FRAUDULENT INSURANCE ACT, WHICH IS A CRIME, AND SHALL ALSO BE SUBJECT TO A CIVIL PENALTY NOT TO EXCEED FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS AND THE STATED VALUE OF THE CLAIM FOR EACH SUCH VIOLATION.
